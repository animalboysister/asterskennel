lesson 3 - modifiers!

vocabulary!

  • vv - compound modifier marker (cmp)
  • cvcvv - not, no, none, nothing
  • cvvcc - some, multiple, many, number
  • cvvcv - all, every, entire, complete
  • cvcvcv - small, short, a little
  • cvcvvc - big, very, important
  • vcvccv - inside, centre, internal
  • cvvcvv - same, equal
  • vccvcc - other, different, change
  • vccvcv - above, top, up, high
  • vccvvc - below, bottom, under, down
  • vcvcvc - near, side, close, nearby
  • vcvcvv - far, away, remove, distance

a modifier is a word that describes or gives more information about another word, like an adjective or adverb in english. modifiers are always placed before the word they modify. these allow compounds to be formed, phrases made up of multiple words that are at the heart of what allows zoophona to express complex ideas.

wawoof woof a wawoof arfuf woofa cvvc v cvcvvc vccvc all animal sbj big good all animals are very good

pronouns can be used as modifiers to form possesives, like 'my', 'your', or 'their' in english.

arf wuff a ufa wuff vcc cvcc v vcv cvcc 1p place sbj 2p place my place is your place

compounds can also be modified by placing a modifier before them.

arf arfuf wrufuf a woorf wawoof vcc v cvccvv cv cvcvvc cvvcc cvvc 1p good feel sbj big big my wellbeing is very important

to use a compound word as a modifier, the compound modifier marker vv is used. the compound modifier is instead placed after the word it modifies and seperated using the compound modifier marker, similarly to the word 'of' in english.

arfuf wrufuf oo arf woof a wawoof wawoof vccvc ccvcvc vv vcc cvvc v cvcvvc cvcvvc good feel cmp 1p animal sbj big big the wellbeing of my animals is very important
