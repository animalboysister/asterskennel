lesson 1 - basic sentences!

vocabulary!

  • v - subject marker (sbj)
  • vcc - 1st person pronoun (1p)
  • vcv - 2nd person pronoun (2p)
  • vvc - 3rd person pronoun (3p)
  • ccvc - this, that, these, those
  • cvcv - say, talk, speak, language
  • cvvc - animal, creature, person
  • ccvv - time, event, period
  • cvcc - place, earth, land, world
  • vccvc - good, positive
  • vccvv - bad, negative, evil
  • vcvcc - move, go, travel, movement
  • vcvcv - want, desire, intention

a sentence in zoophona is made up of at least a verb. a sentence containing only a verb has the meaning of 'there is' that thing. there are no articles such as 'a' or 'the' in zoophona.

woof cvvc animal there is an animal

the word cvcv meaning 'say, talk, or speak' can be used on its own as an interjection to mean 'hello', literally 'there is speaking'.

wufa! cvvc say hello!

the subject of a sentence is the one who performs the verb. in the sentence 'alice loves rover', 'alice' is the subject. in both english and zoophona, the subject is placed before the verb. in zoophona, the subject is seperated from the verb by placing the subject marker v between them.

arf a wawa! vcc v cvcv 1p sbj say I talk

there is no word 'to be' in zoophona. instead, any word can be used as a verb with the meaning of 'to be' or 'to do' that thing.

woof a arfuf! cvvc v vccvc animal sbj good animals are good
